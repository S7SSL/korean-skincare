Complete UK Regulatory Guide for Importing & Selling Online

April 2025 — Post-Brexit Framework

Overview
What you're dealing with and which rules apply

Importing Korean skincare and health supplements into the UK involves two separate regulatory streams. Understanding which category your products fall into is the first critical step.

💄 Cosmetics / Skincare

Regulated by the UK Cosmetics Regulation (OPSS / Office for Product Safety and Standards). Covers creams, serums, toners, masks, cleansers, SPF products.

💊 Supplements / Tablets

Regulated as food products by the Food Standards Agency (FSA), unless medicinal claims are made — then the MHRA applies.

⚠️ Key Risk If a hair growth tablet makes any claim to "treat", "prevent" or "cure" hair loss, it could be classified as a medicine by the MHRA — requiring a full marketing authorisation (very expensive/complex). Stick to general wellness language only.
💄
Part 1: Skincare & Cosmetics
UK Cosmetics Regulation (retained EU law, post-Brexit)

1.1 Appoint a UK Responsible Person (RP)

This is mandatory. Every cosmetic product sold in Great Britain must have a named UK Responsible Person — a UK-based individual or company legally accountable for the product. This person's name and UK address must appear on every label.

  • You (the business) can act as the RP if you are UK-based
  • Or appoint a third-party RP service (costs ~£50–£300 per product per year)
  • The RP is responsible if anything goes wrong — take it seriously

1.2 SCPN — Submit Cosmetic Product Notification

Before any product goes on sale, it must be registered on the UK's SCPN portal (Submit Cosmetic Product Notification). This is free and done online by the RP.

Portal: gov.uk/guidance/cosmetic-product-notifications

  • Each product needs a separate notification
  • EU CPNP notifications do NOT automatically transfer to UK SCPN
  • Registration is instant — no approval wait

1.3 Product Information File (PIF)

You must prepare and maintain a PIF in English for each product. Keep it for 10 years after the last batch is sold. The PIF must contain:

  • Product description — what it is, what it does
  • Cosmetic Product Safety Report (CPSR) — safety assessment by a qualified cosmetic chemist or toxicologist
  • Manufacturing method — evidence of Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP)
  • Efficacy claims proof — evidence for any claims made (e.g. "brightening", "anti-ageing")
  • Certificate of Analysis (CoA) for each ingredient
  • Challenge/stability testing results
💡 Tip The CPSR is the most expensive part. A qualified safety assessor typically charges £150–£500 per product. Korean suppliers may already have safety data — ask for it and get it translated.

1.4 UK Label Requirements

All labels must be in English and include:

  • Name and UK address of the Responsible Person
  • Net content (weight or volume)
  • Date of minimum durability ("best before") or Period After Opening (PAO) symbol
  • Batch number for traceability
  • Product function (what it does)
  • Ingredients list in descending order using INCI names
  • Country of origin (Korea)
  • Precautions and warnings (if applicable)

1.5 Restricted & Prohibited Ingredients

Check every formula against the UK Cosmetics Regulation Annexes. Key 2024/2025 restrictions to watch:

  • Kojic acid — capped at 1% for face and hand products
  • BHT (Butylated Hydroxytoluene) — restricted
  • CMR substances — new restrictions on carcinogens/mutagens
  • Methyl salicylate — restrictions in force from 2025

Korean formulations sometimes contain ingredients restricted in the UK. Check every formula carefully before ordering stock.

1.6 Customs & Import (Cosmetics)

  • HS Code for skincare: typically 3304 (beauty/skin care preparations)
  • Import Duty: 0–6.5% depending on product type
  • VAT: 20% on most cosmetics
  • No specific import licence required for cosmetics (unlike food/medicines)

Part 2: Hair Growth Supplements / Tablets
FSA Food Supplement Regulations — Post-Brexit UK Rules
⚠️ Critical First Step: Classification Check Before doing anything else, confirm the product is a food supplement not a medicine. If it claims to treat or cure hair loss, alopecia, or any medical condition, contact the MHRA for a classification decision. Marketing it incorrectly as a food when it should be a medicine is a criminal offence.

2.1 Register as a Food Business Operator (FBO)

Any business importing food supplements into the UK must register as an FBO with their local authority (council). This is free and straightforward.

  • Register at least 28 days before trading
  • Do this via your local council's environmental health department

2.2 Novel Foods Check

If the supplement contains any ingredient not commonly consumed in Great Britain before May 1997, it is a Novel Food and requires authorisation from the FSA — a lengthy and expensive process (can take 1–2 years).

Common Korean supplement ingredients to check: certain plant extracts, fermented ingredients, marine-derived compounds. Check the FSA Novel Foods catalogue first.

FSA Novel Foods catalogue: food.gov.uk/safety-hygiene/novel-foods

2.3 Customs & Import Controls (Supplements)

Since January 2024, stricter border controls apply. Depending on ingredients:

  • Products of animal origin (e.g. gelatine capsules, collagen, keratin) require export health certificates and pre-notification on IPAFFS
  • Products of plant origin may require phytosanitary certificates
  • Pre-notify via the IPAFFS system before shipment arrives in UK

HS Code for supplements: typically 2106 (food preparations NEC)

Import Duty: typically 0% (but check specific product)

VAT: 0% on most food supplements (zero-rated food)

2.4 UK Label Requirements (Supplements)

Labels must be in English and include:

  • The designation "Food Supplement"
  • List of ingredients with allergens highlighted in bold
  • Name and quantity of active ingredients (vitamins, minerals, etc.)
  • Recommended daily dose — with warning not to exceed it
  • Statement: "Food supplements should not be used as a substitute for a varied diet"
  • Storage instructions + "keep out of reach of children"
  • Best before date and batch number
  • UK importer name and address

2.5 Health Claims

Only authorised health claims from the GB Nutrition and Health Claims Register may be used. You cannot invent your own claims.

  • Acceptable example: "Biotin contributes to the maintenance of normal hair" (authorised claim)
  • Not acceptable: "Regrows hair", "Stops hair loss", "Treats alopecia"
✅ Recommended Approach Focus on ingredients with existing authorised claims — Biotin, Zinc, Iron, Vitamin D. These have pre-approved hair/nail/skin claims and are low-risk. Avoid exotic or unproven Korean-specific ingredients until you've confirmed Novel Food status.

Part 3: Selling Online in the UK
E-commerce compliance requirements

3.1 General E-Commerce Requirements

  • Register as a UK business (sole trader or limited company)
  • Clear terms and conditions, returns policy, privacy policy on website
  • GDPR-compliant data handling (ICO registration — £40/year)
  • Consumer Rights Act compliance (right to return within 14 days for online purchases)

3.2 Marketplace Rules

Amazon UK

  • Cosmetics: submit CPSR, PIF summary, RP details
  • Supplements: FBO registration, lab test results
  • Amazon sometimes requests additional documentation

Your Own Website

  • Full responsibility for compliance
  • Distance selling regulations apply
  • Must display business address and contact details

3.3 Product Liability Insurance

Essential before selling any skincare or supplement product. Protects against claims of injury, allergic reactions, or harm. Budget £200–£800/year for a small operation.

Step-by-Step Launch Checklist
Complete these in order before selling

Phase 1 — Before Ordering Stock

  • Get full ingredient lists (INCI format) for all products from Korean supplier
  • Check all cosmetic ingredients against UK restricted/prohibited list
  • Check all supplement ingredients against FSA Novel Foods catalogue
  • Confirm supplement claims comply with GB Nutrition and Health Claims Register
  • Get MHRA pre-classification opinion if any doubt on supplement category
  • Obtain GMP certificates from Korean manufacturer
  • Obtain Certificate of Analysis (CoA) for each product

Phase 2 — Compliance Setup

  • Register UK business (Companies House or HMRC for sole trader)
  • Register as Food Business Operator with local council (supplements)
  • Appoint UK Responsible Person for cosmetics (self or third party)
  • Commission Cosmetic Product Safety Reports (CPSR) for each skincare product
  • Prepare Product Information Files (PIF) for each cosmetic product
  • Register products on SCPN portal (cosmetics)
  • Get product liability insurance
  • Register with ICO for data protection (£40/year)

Phase 3 — Labelling & Import

  • Design UK-compliant labels in English for all products
  • Arrange relabelling (can be done before import or on arrival)
  • Set up IPAFFS account if importing products of animal/plant origin
  • Obtain required export health certificates from Korean supplier
  • Arrange UK customs clearance agent / freight forwarder
  • Classify products with correct HS codes and confirm duty rates

Phase 4 — Launch

  • Set up e-commerce platform with compliant terms, returns, privacy policy
  • List products with authorised health claims only
  • Test order and verify packaging/labelling on arrival
  • Keep product documentation accessible for 10 years (cosmetics) / 5 years (supplements)
